# Holocure Notes
## Remaining Acheivements
### Bullet Hell Pro
*Clear a Hard stage without taking any damage from projectiles*

For this one I suggest playing Hakos Baelz, mostly because of her character's Special (that allows you to obliterate last boss of Stage 1 on Hard), you would also want to have this character on max G. Rank with all Shop Stat Ups on max.  

For weapons early good to pick Glowstick, Psycho Axe, BL Book, a little bit later going for Plug Type Asacoco and Fan Beam (that you want to upgrade into Dragon Fire). Glowstick can be combined with Idol Song (into Idol Concert) or Wamy Water (into Snow Flower Sake). After those weapons complete you can go for Elite Lava Bucket and Spider Cooking into Elite Cooking.  

On Item build best to get Study Glasses as early as possible, plus Gorilla's Paw for upgrade of Dragon Fire into Holy Fire. Highly suggest taking Kusogaki Shackles (unlocked by surviving for 10 minutes as Murasaki Shion in a single run) that later you wanna build into Injection Type Asacoco, Membership and Face Mask. Note: DO NOT build Injection Type Asacoco before Kusogaki Shackles for obvious (I hope) reasons. Idol Costume is another good Item option that you might wanna pick.  

I suggest using ATK Up Stamp/Projectile Up Stamp with Bomb Stamp and Marking Stamp.  

I would also recommend crafting some food like Tempura (Increase ATK by 30%) or Pufferfish Meal Set (increase ATK/SPD/PUR by 20% and Max HP/Crit/Haste by 10%). Another food option is Turtle Soup which should help you get Study Glasses early.  

If you wanna minimise RNG: Turtle Soup food > early Study Glasses > when Plug Type Asacoco, Fan Beam and Gorilla's Paw appear take them lvl1 if you have choice between lvling something you have or taking new thing. In my run Injection Type Asacoco appeared after I had Kusogaki Shackles on lvl1, in this case you can pick Injection but be very careful and look for fast lvl ups to level Shackles.  

Beware of projectiles at ~16:40 mark! However, it seems that simply running away from where you were fighting when they started to appear and keep running till they stop (it's around 1 minute and 40 seconds of simply running away) works . Best part of doing this on Stage 1 is that this stage is infinite, so you can keep running in any direction you prefer without running into a wall.  
When last boss appears you should have your character Special, if for some reason you don't, keep distance, it's way easier to dodge it the further away from boss those dots are.  

> **Note** (more defensive build option): For weapon Stamps you can go for Pacifist Stamp (on lvl 3: has a 40% chance to destroy enemy projectiles), Projectile Up Stamp (on lvl 2: Adds 2 additional shots to ranged multi-shot weapons), Size Up Stamp (Increase attack size by 25%), which isn't the most reliable way to destroy projectiles, since it's only 40% chance.  
> And for items you can use Body Pillow (gain a shield that absorbs up to 15/20/25/30/35 damage, every 15 seconds, this shield refreshes) and Headphones (15%/20%/25%/30%/35% chance to negate 1 hit, and create a shockwave that knocks back all targets) because it seems like if you take damage with shield instead of your health, it doesn't nullify the progress on achievement. However, I personally do not recommend going for those Stamps or Items, because the way I see it, you would be trading your nuke potential on last boss for something that doesn't make it much easier to get (considering you can tank melee hit for some mob while thinking "oh I can take this projectile with the shield", but melee hit depletes your shield and here you are without damage and without shield; it makes you play sloppier). And you also do not need those for 16:40 mark too, considering you're not supposed to see much of those projectiles other than a couple of those in the corner of the screen. Another thing is that use of those items makes you get less value from Kusogaki Shackles (since Body Pillow and Headphones have no negative effects) and generally last boss fight being longer makes you have to dodge bigger amount of projectiles too, which gives you higher chance to be hit.  
> **Q:** If it's not recommended, when is it still useful if I'm trying to follow dps build listed above?  
> **A:** When you have no other options, no rerolls left and it's almost 20 minutes in the game, so last is about to spawn. You can also give a try to defensive build if you already did couple of attempts with full dps build and didn't like it for some reason. I'm neither your mother nor the Truth in the last instance, you're free to proceed as you wish and as you see fit.  
> If you've decided to go for both defensive options, you can also try picking Ceres Fauna (can be obtained in Shop > Character Gacha > Council) because of her skill Whisperer (on lvl 3: Every 10 seconds, gain 1 Whisper (max 5). Each stack heals 2% HP every 2 seconds. On taking the next hit, consume 1 Whisper to negate the attack).
